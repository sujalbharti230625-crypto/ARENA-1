# Signal Interpretation Guide

## How Signals Are Generated

The terminal uses a **weighted confluence scoring** system based purely on:

1. **Market Structure** (BOS / CHoCH)
2. **Supply & Demand Zone proximity**
3. **Elliott Wave phase** (impulse vs corrective)
4. **USDT Dominance direction** (inverse correlation)
5. **Candlestick patterns** (pin bars, engulfing, etc.)

### Score → Signal

| Score | Signal | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ +2 | 🟢 LONG BIAS | Enter long at demand zone edge |
| ≤ −2 | 🔴 SHORT BIAS | Enter short at supply zone edge |
| −1 to +1 | 🔵 NEUTRAL | No trade — wait for confluence |

## Level Definitions

| Level | Meaning | Position Sizing |
|---|---|---|
| Entry Zone | Price range at demand/supply edge | 100% position entry |
| Stop Loss | Below demand bottom / above supply top | Exit entire position |
| TP1 (50%) | First liquidity pocket | Close 50% of position |
| TP2 (30%) | Mid-range supply/demand | Close 30% of remaining |
| TP3 (20%) | Full target — next structure | Let 20% run with trail |

## Confidence Score

The confidence bar (0–96%) reflects how many of the 7 confluence factors align:

- **>75%** — High conviction, full size entry
- **60–75%** — Medium conviction, reduced size
- **<60%** — Low conviction, paper trade only

## Risk Management Rules

- **Never risk more than 2% of capital per trade**
- Stop Loss is always structural (below/above zone), never arbitrary %
- Only trade in the direction of the HTF (Higher Timeframe) trend
- Require minimum 2:1 RR before entering
